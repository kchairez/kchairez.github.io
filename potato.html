Seed Potato

Rocket Seed Potato

---Growing seed potatoes are the simplest ways to start growing potatos and the concept applies to almost every other kind of potato. Seed potatoes are basically grown healthy potatoes that have sprouts that when planted can easily grow into potato plants. This form of potato growing is recommended as it is much faster than growing from actual true potato seeds. Originally, potatoes were first cultivated by the Incas of Peru and were adopted by the Spanish Conquistadors who introduced the potatoes to Europe. Now potatoes are the 4th most important crop to the world, as they are a very nutrient dense vegetable.

Season: Spring, Summer

Light: Full Sun

Plant Spacing: 24" rows and 10" apart

Matures:70-84 Days

  • Planting begins early spring [March-April] (can even start planting late Spring as well). Ensure that soil temps have reached +45Â°F or wait 2 weeks after the last frost of the season in the area.
  • Plant individual seed potatoes in drills 6" deep, 10" apart, and 24" apart from other rows in garden grade, well drained open soil with the approximate pH levels of 6.0 - 6.5.
  • Ensure when planting the potatoes that their eyes are facing upwards (The sprouts coming out their skins).
  • Be sure to apply water well when there are dry spells.
  • Harvest begins in June - July or late July/ early August if you planted late Spring (10-12 weeks for maturity). Dig up the mature potatoes and store them in a cool, dry, and dark location. Potatoes can last several months when stored properly. More info
